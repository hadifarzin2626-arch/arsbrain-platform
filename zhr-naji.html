ZHR NAJI PROTECTION ARCHITECTURE

A Layered Architecture Built Around Protection.

ZHR NAJI is designed as a unified intelligent protection layer within the ARS ecosystem.

It brings together sensing, awareness, intelligence, communication and response into one coordinated architecture designed to help protect people before, during and after critical situations.

One Protection Layer. Multiple Layers Of Intelligence.

Protection is never the result of one sensor, one camera or one algorithm.

Real protection requires multiple layers working together.

ZHR NAJI is therefore designed as a coordinated architecture in which every layer has a specific responsibility while remaining connected to the larger protection system.

The goal is simple: Detect earlier. Understand better. Respond faster.

THE PROTECTION LAYERS

Seven Layers. One Protection Philosophy.

ZHR NAJI is built around a layered protection model designed to create resilience, redundancy and intelligent coordination.

01
◉

SENSING

The First Layer Of Awareness.

ZHR NAJI receives information from relevant sensors and vehicle systems designed to observe the environment and the condition of the vehicle and its occupants.

Depending on the vehicle and configuration, this may include cameras, parking sensors, blind-spot detection, TPMS, temperature sensors, light and rain sensors and other supported inputs.

02
◌

PERCEPTION

Turning Data Into Understanding.

Raw sensor information is not enough.

The system must interpret relevant information and understand what may be happening around the vehicle and its occupants.

The perception layer transforms multiple inputs into meaningful environmental and situational awareness.

03
◈

INTELLIGENCE

Understanding What Matters.

The intelligence layer analyzes relevant information and helps determine whether a condition may require attention.

It is designed to distinguish between normal conditions, potential risks and situations that may require a higher level of awareness.

The objective is not to create unnecessary alerts.

The objective is to create meaningful awareness.

04
△

RISK ASSESSMENT

Understanding The Level Of Risk.

Not every detected event has the same importance.

ZHR NAJI is designed around the concept of contextual risk assessment, where relevant information is evaluated according to the situation and available evidence.

This helps prioritize events and reduce unnecessary distractions.

05
!

AWARENESS & ALERT

When Awareness Becomes Action.

When a meaningful risk is identified, ZHR NAJI can communicate appropriate warnings through supported interfaces.

The goal is to make important information understandable, timely and actionable.

Warnings should be clear enough to help people respond without creating unnecessary fear or confusion.

06
◐

ASSISTANCE

Support When People Need It.

When a person requires assistance, ZHR NAJI is designed to provide an additional layer of support through intelligent guidance and connected services where available.

This may include communication with trusted contacts or other authorized support pathways.

07
✦

RESPONSE

Supporting The Next Step.

The final layer focuses on helping people move from awareness toward an appropriate response.

Depending on the situation and available capabilities, this may involve user guidance, family notification, emergency communication or other authorized support mechanisms.

ZHR NAJI does not replace professional emergency services.

SAFETY PRINCIPLE

Fail-Safe Thinking.

A system designed to support safety must also be designed to fail responsibly.

ZHR NAJI is built around the principle that no single component should be treated as infallible.

Sensors can fail.

Cameras can become unavailable.

Communication can be interrupted.

Software can encounter unexpected conditions.

For this reason, the architecture must be designed to recognize limitations, communicate uncertainty and avoid creating a false sense of security.

When the system cannot reliably know, it must not pretend that it does.

This principle is fundamental to responsible intelligent protection.

PRIVACY BY DESIGN

Protection Must Respect Privacy.

A system designed to protect people must also respect their dignity and privacy.

ZHR NAJI is intended to follow a privacy-conscious architecture in which data collection, processing, storage and communication are governed by clear principles and appropriate authorization.

Not every piece of information needs to leave the vehicle.

Not every event needs to be stored forever.

Not every system needs access to every piece of data.

The long-term objective is to minimize unnecessary data exposure while preserving the information required for legitimate safety and assistance functions.

Protection should never require unnecessary loss of dignity.

MODULAR BY DESIGN

Built To Evolve.

The world of technology changes continuously.

A protection system designed today must be capable of evolving tomorrow.

ZHR NAJI is therefore designed around modularity.

New sensors.

New cameras.

New algorithms.

New communication systems.

New accessibility technologies.

New forms of intelligent assistance.

The architecture should allow future capabilities to be introduced without requiring the entire protection ecosystem to be redesigned from the beginning.

Protection should evolve with technology, without losing its purpose.
